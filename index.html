National Guideline for
Clinical Management of Dengue

The 5th Edition (2025) of the National Guideline was developed by the Communicable Disease Control Division, DGHS — in collaboration with WHO Bangladesh and national experts. New additions include updated paediatric management and a comprehensive chapter on dengue in pregnancy.

Aims

  • Comprehensive understanding of dengue
  • Diagnosis & management at all tiers of care
  • Uniform use of available tools
  • Customised to Bangladesh context

Intended For

  • Clinicians caring for dengue patients
  • Programme personnel
  • Health care managers & policymakers
  • Medical students & residents

2023 Largest Ever Outbreak

  • 321,179 confirmed cases; 1,705 deaths
  • CFR 0.53% — one of highest globally
  • 74% deaths due to DSS; 81% within 3 days of admission
  • Peak: Sep 2023 — 79,598 cases, 396 deaths

2024 Update

  • 101,214 admitted; 575 deaths
  • 19.3% cases in children (0–15 yrs)
  • Den-2 dominant (70%), Den-3 (20%), Den-4 (9%)
  • Now year-round nationwide — no longer seasonal
ℹ️
Up to 80–90% of dengue infections are asymptomatic. Only <5% progress to severe forms. These unreported cases drive silent community transmission.

Dengue pathogenesis involves complex virus–host interaction. Severe disease — especially in secondary heterotypic infection — is driven by:

  • ADE (Antibody-dependent enhancement) — non-neutralising antibodies ↑ viral biomass
  • Cytokine storm — CD4+/CD8+ T cells → IFN-γ, TNF-α, lymphotoxin
  • Complement activation — DENV NS1 disrupts endothelium via TLR4
  • Vasculopathy → capillary permeability → plasma leakage → haemoconcentration, effusions, DSS
  • Coagulopathy → ↑aPTT, ↓fibrinogen, DIC, thrombocytopenia
  • Cytopathy → leucopenia, thrombocytopenia; Organopathy → MOF
⚠️
Secondary infection with DENV-2 or multiple serotypes significantly increases DHF/DSS risk. DENV-2 is predominant in Bangladesh 2024 (70%).

Classic DF has NO critical phase. Critical phase in DHF starts around Day 3–5 at defervescence and lasts only 24–48 hrs. Identifying its onset and end is the cornerstone of safe fluid management. An equilibrium phase may occur before convalescence.

🟢 Without Warning Signs

Fever 2–7 days + 2 of:

  • Nausea/vomiting
  • Rash
  • Aches & pains
  • Tourniquet test (+)
  • Leucopenia WBC <5000

🟡 With Warning Signs ⚠️

  • Intense abdominal pain
  • Persistent vomiting ≥3/hr or ≥4/6hr
  • Fluid accumulation
  • Mucosal bleeding
  • Altered GCS <15
  • Hepatomegaly ≥2cm
  • Progressive ↑HCT (×2 readings, 6hr apart)

🔴 Severe Dengue

  • Severe plasma leakage → DSS or respiratory distress
  • Severe bleeding (clinical eval)
  • Severe organ involvement — AST/ALT ≥1000, impaired consciousness, organ failure
High-Risk Groups — Admit Even Without Warning Signs
  • Pregnant women
  • Elderly (>65 yrs)
  • Obesity
  • Diabetes mellitus
  • Hypertension, CAD, heart failure
  • Chronic renal / liver disease
  • Haemolytic diseases, thalassaemia
  • Immunocompromised
  • On steroids, NSAIDs, anticoagulants
  • Adverse social circumstances

✅ DO

  • Adequate bed rest under mosquito net
  • Oral fluids (ORS, coconut water, fruit juice, soup): 2400 ml/day (2 ml/kg/hr up to 50 kg)
  • Paracetamol only: max 3 g/day adults; 60 mg/kg/24hr children
  • Tepid sponging
  • Monitor urine every 3–4 hrs
  • Review daily with CBC from Day 3 (with SGOT/SGPT)
  • Avoid dark-coloured food/drinks (confusion with melaena)

❌ AVOID

  • Aspirin, Mefenamic acid, Ibuprofen, NSAIDs, COX-2 inhibitors
  • Steroids
  • Antibiotics (unless proven co-infection)
  • IM injections
  • Plain water only (use electrolyte fluids)
  • Warfarin/aspirin/clopidogrel — temporarily withhold

Holliday-Segar Maintenance

  • 1st 10 kg → 100 ml/kg/day
  • 2nd 10 kg → 50 ml/kg/day
  • Each kg beyond 20 → 20 ml/kg/day

50 kg Adult Example

  • 1st 10 kg = 1000 ml
  • 2nd 10 kg = 500 ml
  • 20–50 kg = 600 ml → Maint = 2100 ml
  • 5% deficit = 2500 ml
  • Total = 4600 ml (48 hrs)
💡
HCT response rule: Crystalloid 500 ml → HCT ↓1–2 pts (if >3 pts → suspect bleeding). Dextran 500 ml → HCT ↓8–10 pts (if more → bleeding). Blood transfusion → HCT ↑3–5 pts per unit.
Non-Shock Critical Stage: Start at 40 ml/hr (adults). Reduce: 7→5→3→1.5 ml/kg/hr as stable → stop 24–48 hrs.

Crystalloids (First Choice)

  • 0.9% NaCl (NS) — preferred
  • Hartmann's solution — preferred
  • 5% DRL / 5% DRA
  • 0.45% NS — children <6 months

Colloids (Rescue)

  • Dextran-40 (max 30 ml/kg/day)
  • 6% HES (Voluven/Plasmasol)
  • Human Albumin, Plasma, Haemaccel
  • Blood & blood components
⚠️
Colloids indicated: Shock not responding to 2 crystalloid boluses with rising HCT · Fluid quota nearly exceeded · Shock + fluid overload · Pulse pressure <10 mmHg

🚨
Profound shock (no BP/pulse): NS 10–20 ml/kg bolus (500 ml adults) in 15–30 min + O₂ · NaHCO₃ 1–2 mEq/kg if acidosis · No response → Colloid → Blood → ABCS → plan dialysis/organ support
Refractory Shock — ABCS

A = Acidosis

VBG + serum lactate

B = Bleeding

HCT; suspect concealed haemorrhage

C = Calcium

Serum Ca²⁺, Na⁺, K⁺

S = Sugar

Capillary blood glucose

Suspect Severe Bleeding When:

  • Unstable haemodynamics regardless of HCT level
  • HCT drops after fluid bolus + unstable vitals
  • Refractory shock after 40–60 ml/kg fluid
  • Hypotension + inappropriately low/normal HCT
  • Persistent metabolic acidosis

Blood Transfusion Indicated:

  • Overt bleeding >10% or 6–8 ml/kg
  • HCT drop <40% (<45% males) after resuscitation
  • Hypotensive shock + low/normal HCT
  • Refractory shock after 40–60 ml/kg
⚠️
Platelet transfusion — very limited role. Fresh whole blood usually sufficient. Only for: very severe thrombocytopenia needing urgent surgery, or clinical judgement. FFP/platelet concentrate for severe bleeding may worsen fluid overload.

Calcium

10% Ca gluconate 10 ml over 10 min; repeat 6-hrly × 24 hrs. Indications: shock not responding to 2 boluses, any shock, >2 units PRC, myocarditis.

Glucose

Both hyper- and hypoglycaemia occur. Monitor 1–2 hrly. Hypoglycaemia: 0.1–0.5 g/kg glucose IV (not in resuscitation fluid). Target BSL 80–100 mg/dl.

Electrolytes

Hyponatraemia (most common) — isotonic fluid prevents/corrects. Hypokalaemia — KCl in parenteral fluid. Metabolic acidosis — correct shock first; NaHCO₃ NOT for pH ≥7.10.

Signs of Recovery

  • Stable pulse, BP, breathing rate
  • Normal temperature
  • No external/internal bleeding
  • Return of appetite; no vomiting/abdominal pain
  • Good urinary output
  • Stable HCT at baseline
  • Convalescent rash / itching extremities

Discharge Criteria

  • No fever ≥24 hrs (without antipyretics)
  • ≥2 days after recovery from shock
  • Good appetite, improving nutrition
  • Normal HCT at baseline (or 38–40% if unknown)
  • No pleural effusion distress / no ascites
  • Platelet >50,000/mm³
  • No complications
  • Higher body water content → more prone to fluid shifts during plasma leakage
  • Smaller blood volume (80 ml/kg) → minor plasma loss causes major haemodynamic instability
  • More prone to fluid overload — smaller CV and renal capacity
  • Younger children cannot express symptoms clearly → warning signs missed
  • Abdominal symptoms more common than adults
  • Co-infections common — cough/coryza does NOT exclude dengue
  • Negative tourniquet test does NOT exclude dengue
📊
In Bangladesh 2024, 19.3% of dengue cases were children aged 0–15 years (up from 17% in 2023). No age is immune.

Monitor every 15 minutes in shock. Check HCT before and 10–15 min after each bolus. Insert urinary catheter if shock prolonged — hourly UOP.
Discharge Criteria (Children)
  • No fever ≥24 hrs (without antipyretics); ≥2 days after recovery from shock
  • Normal HCT at baseline (or 38–40%); Platelet >50,000/mm³
  • Good appetite; no vomiting/abdominal pain; good urinary output
  • No pleural effusion distress; no ascites; no other complications

Physiological Challenges

  • HCT rise masked by haemodilution (↑plasma vol 2nd/3rd trimester)
  • Gravid uterus — third-space fluid hard to detect
  • Baseline BP lower, pulse pressure wider, HR higher
  • Lower HCO₃ baseline
  • Serial HCT monitoring crucial

Impact on Fetus/Pregnancy

  • Early abortion (3–13%)
  • Preterm birth (3–33%)
  • Low birth weight (9–16%)
  • IUD/stillbirth (4.7–13%)
  • APH, PPH, increased C/S incidence
  • Vertical transmission 1.6–10.5%
⚠️
All pregnant women with acute fever MUST be admitted — regardless of platelet count — for close monitoring by obstetric and medical teams (MDT). Tertiary care for DSS/DHF with complications.

💡
In pregnant women, anticipate leaking when platelets drop to ≤130 × 10⁹/L (lower threshold than non-pregnant). Perform HCT 3-hourly from this point. Fluid quota calculated on pre-pregnancy / early-pregnancy body weight.

✅ DO

  • Hospital delivery; blood available; MDT (Obstetrician, Internist, Anaesthetist, Neonatologist)
  • Active management of 3rd stage of labour (AMTSL) + Oxytocin infusion post-delivery
  • Misoprostol/Tranexamic acid 1 g IV (repeat if bleeding >30 min) for PPH
  • Prophylactic balloon tamponade if delivery during critical phase
  • Encourage breastfeeding; early cord clamping
  • Cord blood: CBC, NS1, Dengue Ab, blood group

❌ AVOID

  • Medical termination / MR / D&C / amniocentesis in acute dengue
  • Elective C/S or induction of labour during critical phase
  • Episiotomy (unless inevitable delivery)
  • Instrumental delivery
  • IM injections / skin pricks for glucose
  • Prophylactic platelets (unless plt <50,000 & delivery in 6 hrs)

Dengue Encephalopathy

  • IV fluid ≤80% maintenance; switch to colloid if large vol + leakage
  • Head up 30°; early intubation if needed
  • IV Mannitol; Dexamethasone 0.15 mg/kg IV q6–8h
  • Lactulose 5–10 ml q6h; blood glucose 80–100 mg/dl
  • Vitamin K₁ IV; anticonvulsants as indicated

Dengue Myocarditis

  • Troponin I, CK-MB, ECG, Echocardiogram
  • Myocardial dysfunction does NOT preclude fluid management
  • Empirical calcium (hypocalcaemia common in large effusions)
  • Avoid fluid overload (→ pulmonary oedema)
  • Dobutamine/milrinone for low cardiac output

HLH (Hemophagocytic Lymphohistiocytosis)

  • Suspect: fever >6 days + worsening cytopaenia + organomegaly
  • HLH-2004 criteria (≥5 of 8); ferritin ≥500 μg/L
  • Dexamethasone IV 10 mg/m²/day → oral taper
  • IV Immunoglobulin 0.75 g/kg stat + 0.4 g/kg × 3 days
  • Etoposide as rescue therapy

Fluid Overload Management

  • Stop IV fluid if not in shock
  • Furosemide IV: 1 mg/kg adults (40 mg); 0.1 mg/kg child
  • Dextran-40: 10 ml/kg bolus with furosemide midway
  • Insert urinary catheter; hourly UOP
  • No urine → check CVP/lactate → consider dialysis
  • Pleural/peritoneal tapping if severe respiratory distress

✅ DO

  • Paracetamol for fever/myalgia
  • Haemodynamic assessment before & after every fluid bolus
  • IV fluids for repeated vomiting or rapidly rising HCT
  • Appropriate isotonic IV fluids in correct time and dose
  • Tight glycaemic control
  • Colloid/PCV/FWB if indicated
  • Notify public health authorities about suspected dengue

❌ DON'T

  • Send home without follow-up instructions
  • Give NSAIDs (aspirin, ibuprofen, mefenamic acid)
  • Give IV fluids to mild dengue who can take orally
  • Give excessive fluid, blood, blood products
  • Give antibiotics for dengue alone
  • Change IV infusion rate rapidly/abruptly
  • Insert NGT to check concealed bleeding
Bedside Quick Tips
  • Leucopenia <5000/mm³ → patient will become afebrile within 24 hrs and enter critical phase
  • Hb × 3 ≈ HCT level
  • Sudden pallor = internal bleeding
  • Surface/mild bleeding → HCT higher. Deep/massive bleeding → HCT lower
  • Sudden haemodynamic deterioration refractory to fluid → suspect blood loss
  • Haemorrhage during febrile phase = DF with unusual haemorrhage, probably NOT DHF
  • Haemorrhage without fever → critically assess for DHF

Clinical Criteria

  • Profound shock despite all inpatient management
  • DIC with uncontrolled bleeding
  • Multiorgan failure (≥2 organs)

Severe Metabolic Abnormalities

  • pH <7.25 or HCO₃ <15 mmol/L after 2 crystalloid + 1 colloid bolus
  • Severe hypoglycaemia <70 mg/dl
  • Severe hyponatraemia <124 mmol/L
  • Severe hypocalcaemia <2.1 mmol/L

Indications for MV

  • ARDS — PaO₂ <60 mmHg on high-flow O₂
  • PaCO₂ >50 mmHg + respiratory acidosis
  • RR >35/min with respiratory fatigue
  • Hypotension unresponsive to fluid (lactate >4 mmol/L)
  • Encephalopathy with loss of airway reflexes
  • Massive GI bleeding / haemoptysis

Lung-Protective Strategy (ARDS)

  • Tidal volume: 4–6 ml/kg ideal body weight
  • Plateau pressure <30 cmH₂O
  • SpO₂ >92%; avoid hyperoxia
  • Adjust PEEP to oxygenation
  • Consider prone positioning in severe ARDS
ICU Discharge Criteria
  • MAP ≥65 mmHg without vasopressors; adequate CRFT; normal HR
  • HCT normalised; no effusions on exam/USG
  • No active bleeding ≥24–48 hrs; platelet ≥50,000
  • UOP >0.5 ml/kg/hr without RRT; no encephalopathy
  • Stable oxygenation on minimal/no supplemental O₂
  • Able to tolerate oral intake; no ICU-level complications

  • Cover all water storage containers tightly to prevent egg-laying by Aedes mosquito
  • Empty and dry water tanks, coolers, flower pots, pet water bowls, plant drip trays at least once weekly
  • Discard waste articles (tyres etc.) that hold stagnant water
  • Check gutters and flat roofs regularly for blockage
  • Spray commercially available pyrethroid aerosols; close room 15–20 min
  • Spray timing: early morning or late afternoon (Ae. aegypti peak biting time)
  • Wear full-sleeved clothing during daytime; use commercially available repellents
  • Use insecticide-treated mosquito nets during day sleep
  • Fit doors and windows with mosquito screens/wire mesh
  • Introduce larvivorous fish (Gambusia/Guppy) in ornamental water tanks
  • Designate a nodal officer for weekly larval habitat inspection at institutions
  • Ensure overhead tanks have mosquito-proof lids and screened overflow pipes
  • Organise dry day campaigns — empty all containers weekly
  • Carry out indoor space spraying with pyrethrum 2% or cyphenothrin
  • Promote personal protective measures in community
  • Report all fever cases (suspected dengue) to local health authorities
  • Apply temephos granules (1 ppm) when containers cannot be emptied
  • Mobilise households to cooperate during spraying/fogging campaigns
